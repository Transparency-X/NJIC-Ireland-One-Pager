# National Justice Integrity Commission (NJIC) — Ireland

## One-Page Summary: What It Is & What It Is Not

---

### THE PROBLEM

Ireland has no independent, cross-cutting body to detect systemic integrity failures across its justice system. Oversight is fragmented:

| Agency | Overseen By | Gap |
|--------|-------------|-----|
| An Garda Síochána | PCSA, GSOC/IOPO | No cross-system pattern analysis |
| Irish Prison Service | Inspector of Prisons | No corruption-risk benchmarking |
| Courts Service | Judicial Council | No external transparency scoring |
| Probation Service | Internal / Ministerial | No independent integrity monitoring |
| Legal Profession | Legal Services Regulatory Authority | No justice-sector-wide accountability |

**Result:** Corruption risks, procurement irregularities, and systemic misconduct patterns that span multiple agencies go undetected until they become crises.

---

### WHAT THE NJIC IS

| | |
|:---|:---|
| **A statutory or statutory-style commission** | Independent of the agencies it monitors; funded through the Oireachtas or EU anti-corruption frameworks. |
| **Cross-system** | Covers An Garda Síochána, Irish Prison Service, Courts Service, Probation Service, and the legal profession — as a single ecosystem. |
| **Integrity-focused** | Benchmarks transparency, ethical conduct, accountability, and corruption risk — not day-to-day operational management. |
| **Evidence-based** | Publishes annual integrity scores, risk heatmaps, and policy recommendations derived from open data, FOI responses, and validated official sources. |
| **Advocacy-enabling** | Provides researchers, journalists, TDs, and civil society with structured, citable datasets to support reform. |
| **Whistleblower-protective** | Maintains secure, anonymised channels for reporting cross-cutting integrity concerns without storing identities. |

---

### WHAT THE NJIC IS NOT

| | |
|:---|:---|
| **❌ A police force** | It has no powers of arrest, search, or seizure. |
| **❌ A court or tribunal** | It does not determine guilt, impose sanctions, or adjudicate individual cases. |
| **❌ An operational manager** | It does not set policing priorities, prison budgets, court schedules, or probation targets. |
| **❌ A replacement for existing oversight bodies** | It does not duplicate the PCSA (Garda performance), GSOC/IOPO (Garda complaints), the Inspector of Prisons, or the Judicial Council. |
| **❌ A prosecutorial body** | It does not bring criminal charges or disciplinary proceedings. |
| **❌ A leaks platform** | It does not publish unverified allegations; all data is sourced, documented, and subject to provenance logging. |

---

### RELATIONSHIP TO EXISTING BODIES

| Existing Body | What It Does | What NJIC Adds |
|---------------|--------------|----------------|
| **PCSA** | Garda performance oversight, inspections, community safety | Cross-system integrity benchmarking; corruption-risk analysis spanning Garda-Prison-Court nexus |
| **GSOC / IOPO** | Investigates individual Garda complaints | Identifies systemic misconduct patterns across agencies; complements but does not investigate |
| **Inspector of Prisons** | Prison conditions, human rights compliance | Prison-specific integrity scoring (corruption, procurement, staff conduct) |
| **Judicial Council** | Judicial conduct, appointments, education | External transparency index for judicial accountability |
| **C&AG** | Public financial audit | Sector-specific procurement risk mapping |
| **DPP** | Prosecution decisions | Prosecution transparency metrics (consistency, disclosure compliance) |

> **Key Principle:** The NJIC uses existing bodies' published outputs as *inputs* for its cross-system analysis. It fills the gap between single-agency oversight and whole-system integrity.

---

### THE ASK

Establish a **National Justice Integrity Commission** — either as a new statutory body or a formally recognised independent framework — to:

1. **Monitor** integrity risks across all justice agencies using a standardised 5-dimension methodology.
2. **Report** annually to the Oireachtas and the public with scored, benchmarked, peer-reviewed findings.
3. **Recommend** evidence-based reforms to legislation, regulation, and agency practice.
4. **Protect** whistleblowers who expose cross-system integrity failures.

---

### IN ONE SENTENCE

> **The NJIC is Ireland's missing "single pair of eyes" on justice system integrity — independent, cross-cutting, evidence-based, and complementary to existing operational oversight bodies.**
